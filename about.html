Louis L. Reed

Lived truth turned into public leadership.

After serving 14 years in federal prison, Louis reentered public life and became one of the leading architects behind the First Step Act, helping shape one of the most significant criminal justice reforms in recent history.

His work now spans executive advisory, keynote speaking, public narrative, and documentary storytelling, always grounded in credibility rather than performance.
